California Senate 2024

Candidates

Background Info

Adam Schiff was born on June 22, 1960 in Massachusetts to parents who were holocaust immigrants, graduating from Monte Vista High School and went to Stanford University for a political science degree, and became a doctor of law from Harvard law school.

Political Involvement

He was a member of the California State Senate from 1996 to 2000, then he was a member of the House of Representatives representing California from 2001. During this time he led the impeachment of President Donald Trump, and was an active member of the January 6th Committee.

Background Info

Steve Garvey was born on December 22, 1948 in Florida to Irish parents. After graduating from Chamberlain High School Garvey went to play baseball at Michigan State University. He would go on to play in major league baseball for the Los Angeles Dodgers.

Political Involvement

Garvey has no prior political experience.

Political Stance

Education

Schiff believes in making public college free, in an attempt to try to increase the workforce. Expand Pell grants. Cancel Student debts from every borrower for up to $50,000, then also completing the debts of those who are disabled and had debts lasting past 20 years by passing an Act.

Garvey wants to Maximize education funding, support school choice, and focus on fundamentals. Specific actions include, giving tax credits for donations towards K-12 education, expanding funding for schools which the Biden Administration has cut off, and extending a tax rule that gives parents money concerning K-12 schooling.

Economy

Schiff wants to lower inflation by addressing the scarcity that exists within the economy. This would be done through means of government intervention in the form of regulations and the passing of acts that provide more social benefits. Schiff does not believe in a blanket tariff as he states that this will lead to inflation. However, Schiff does believe in targeted tariffs against China, when they try to kill American corporations. (Do it strategically, but not indiscriminately). Schiff believes that our tax code is unfair to working people and middle income families. He wants to change that by eliminating the Trump tax cut. He would support child tax credits, and make the very wealthy pay their fair share.

Garvey wants to stop inflation mainly by opposing government spending and allowing the market to correct itself. He believes that by lower taxes and regulations the cost of living will lower. He believes in the elimination of regulations as it stifles the economy, which then limits growth. Free market capitalization supply side. Garvey believed that tariffs should be considered if the company overseas is a threat to a domestic company, but he does not believe that tariffs are the answer. Lower taxes is the key to not needing imports from foreign countries. Garvey believes that before the Biden administration, the tax was in the perfect spot. He wants taxation to allow small businesses to thrive. “I’m good with the Trump tax”, Garvey says, he thinks that it works and the Biden administration did a bad thing.

Border

Schiff believes in a strong border policy, which means more personal and better technology, both people and narcotics. He wants a immigration policy that treats those who are immigrating as human beings, and provides relief for farmers. People who actually want to aid the US and actually make their living in the US should be treated with Respect and dignity. Schiff believes that a strong border and treating people as human beings does not have to be contradictory. Schiff says that most fentanyl carried through ports of entry by Americans, go after them, go after cartels, go after China, go after manufacturers.

Garvey wants to secure the border, “finish off the wall”, reinstate “remain in Mexico”, reinforce border control, building facilities along the border to detain illegal immigrants, and a judicial system that will try the cases. Dealing with the issue of fentanyl is first securing the border, dealing with Mexico honestly and straight forward, and dealing with China. Stop being nice with Mexico and China, people are being killed, Garvey says.

Housing

Building housing, incentivising the building of new housing. Schiff believes in government intervention in incentivising the building of new housing: making a federal investment in housing. Also incentivise local government agencies to make quicker decisions on housing. Schiff believes that it is unfair that people who rent cannot write off their rent like people with a mortgage on a house can, thus he wants to see a renters tax credit, so renters can get some help. Expanding section 8 vouture, which are originally intended for veterans. Not more government regulations, but rather incentives and a speedy response

Working with builders to give incentive to build housing. Garvey believes that the housing issue in California does not concern the national government and should instead be worked out on a local level. For the issue of renting Garvey believes that as long as the economy goes supply side and there is less government intervention, there will be more affordable housing, and people will be able to rent at an affordable rate. Definitely no government regulations.

Health Care

Schiff wants to make Medicare available to everyone who wants it, and people who don’t want to participate shouldn’t have to either. Continue to negotiate down the price of medication, continuing the fight of social security. He wants to make sure that future generations will have health care.

Garvey wants a strong Medical, Medicaid, and extended medicare. Not do away with the competitive nature of medication companies. Free market capitalization, where the healthcare companies can compete naturally to benefit the consumer. Garvey believes that these companies’ competition is restricted by regulations.
